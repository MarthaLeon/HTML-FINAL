WILDLIFE IN SOUTH AMERICA

Wildlife is being defined as undomesticated animal species and the ones that grow or live wild in an area without being introduced by humans. They

live in different ecosystems like deserts, plains,grasslands, woodlands, forest as well as in the most developed urban areas. Nowadays wildlife is

considered as valuable to human beings economically, educationally and emotionally in nature. In South America, we can find different endemic

species of wildlife. Next you will find a brief description of some of them:

ANACONDA

Considered the heaviest snake in the world and also known as water boas, they can grow up to 9.15m large and weigh up to 250 kg. Constrictors that

wrap their bodies tight around the prey to break the bones and make the heart to stop after which they loosen their grip and start to swallow their prey.

CAPYBARA

Considered the largest rodent in the world can grow up to around 0.6 m tall and 1.2 m long. They are covered with brown shaggy fur. They can stay

underwater for up to five minutes at a time to hide from predators.

PIRANHA

Considered the largest rodent in the world can grow up to around 0.6 m tall and 1.2 m long. They are covered with brown shaggy fur. They can stay

underwater for up to five minutes at a time to hide from predators.

JAGUAR

Considered the third largest cat in the world whose name came from the Tupi and Guaraní language which means “true, fierce beast”, can grow up to

2.4 m from the nose to the tip of the tail and can weight up to 136 kg. They prey on small and medium-sized animals whose skull is pierced with their

sharp canines.

ANDEAN BEAR

Considered the largest land carnivore in South America can weight up to 200 kg and grow up to 150 cm. They perceive from the ground when a tree is

loaded with ripe fruit.They build platforms to reach food high in trees or nests to sleep in. It is a vulnerable species which is being investigated and

protected because of it. We have to understand the natural world to be able to protect the wildlife and wildplaces we all care about. Their intrinsec value

and the fact that they are vital to ecosystem health makes us to believe that by saving them we can:

  • Protect all other biodiversity
  • Develop policies and solutions that benefit nature and business
  • Combat diseases that threaten the health of people, domestic animals and wildlife
